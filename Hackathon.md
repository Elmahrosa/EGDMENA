# The Elmahrosa Global Day 2026 Hackathon Journey
EGD2026 Hackathon Journey

### Learn. Build. Compete. Grow.

### An Elmahrosa Global Day 2026 Learning & Innovation Program
### Incorporating Learning Resources Available Through the Anthropic Partner Academy (APA)

### One Day. Many Cities. One MENA & Africa AI Community.

From registration to certification opportunities — here's the complete path for EGD2026 participants.

---

## Why Join EGD2026?

EGD2026 combines structured learning, hands-on building, and real recognition in one focused program. Participants gain practical skills in responsible AI development, compete in a regional hackathon, and have the opportunity to earn industry-recognized certification support.

---

## Definitions

- **EGD2026** means Elmahrosa Global Day 2026.
- **Required Learning Phase** means the learning curriculum established by Elmahrosa for hackathon eligibility.
- **APA** refers to the Anthropic Partner Academy.
- **CPN** refers to the Claude Partner Network.
- **CCAF** refers to the Claude Certified Architect – Foundations certification.
- **Participant** means an individual accepted into the EGD2026 program.

---

## Participant Eligibility

Participation is subject to the eligibility criteria published by Elmahrosa, including any applicable age, residency, educational, professional, or registration requirements. Additional eligibility conditions may apply to specific challenges or prizes.

---

## Step 1 — Registration

Registration opens through the official **Elmahrosa Global Day 2026 (EGD2026)** website, with two tracks:

- **Students** — free registration after student status verification.
- **Professionals** — registration through one of the available ticket categories on the official site.

---

## Step 2 — Admission Review

Every applicant submits a short analytical report on **TEOS Sentinel Shield** as part of the EGD2026 admissions process. This report is an **Elmahrosa requirement** and demonstrates the applicant’s understanding of AI security, governance, and responsible deployment.

The program committee reviews each registration and report. Qualifying applicants are notified with full participation instructions.

---

## Step 3 — Official Elmahrosa Account

Each accepted participant will receive the credentials or account information necessary to access EGD2026 learning materials, communications, and hackathon services.

---

## Step 4 — Required Learning Phase (Complete Within Seven (7) Days of Acceptance)

The Required Learning Phase consists of learning modules designated by Elmahrosa and may include selected learning resources available through the Anthropic Partner Academy (APA). The specific curriculum for each participant will be published on the official registration portal and must be completed within seven (7) days of acceptance.

**Example Learning Paths (subject to final curriculum):**

- Claude Certified Associate – Foundations
- Claude Certified Developer – Foundations
- Claude Certified Architect – Foundations (CCAF)
- Claude Certified Architect – Professional

*(Learning path links will be provided on the registration page.)*

The Required Learning Phase must be completed before a participant is eligible for the hackathon.

**Important:** Completion of learning resources does not constitute certification. Certifications are awarded solely by Anthropic upon successful completion of applicable examinations and satisfaction of any eligibility requirements.

The EGD2026 Required Learning Phase (including the TEOS Sentinel Shield report) is an admission requirement established by Elmahrosa and should not be interpreted as a requirement of Anthropic or the Anthropic Partner Academy.

---

## Step 5 — Hackathon Eligibility

Once the Required Learning Phase is complete, the participant is eligible to enter the **Elmahrosa Global Day 2026 Hackathon**.

---

## Step 6 — The Hackathon

Participants may use Claude models and other AI tools or technologies permitted under the published challenge rules. Any disclosure requirements regarding AI-assisted development will be described in the official Judging Rules. Projects may be developed individually or in teams. Projects are evaluated by the official EGD2026 judging panel using the published evaluation criteria.

📅 **September 15, 2026**

---

## Step 7 — Winner Selection

Up to thirty (30) eligible winners may be selected from each participating country, subject to the quality of submissions and compliance with program rules.

**Founding countries:**
- 🇪🇬 Egypt
- 🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia
- 🇯🇴 Jordan

Additional participating countries may be announced before EGD2026.

🗓️ Winners are judged and announced in **October 2026**, shortly after the hackathon.

---

## Step 8 — The Prize

Selected winners may be eligible to receive an Elmahrosa-sponsored voucher to cover the registration fee for the **Claude Certified Architect – Foundations (CCAF)** certification exam.

**Important Note:** Redemption of the CCAF exam voucher is subject to Anthropic’s Claude Partner Network (CPN) eligibility requirements in effect at the time of redemption. Elmahrosa will provide guidance to eligible winners and, where possible, support the redemption process.

## Participant Responsibilities

Participants are responsible for:

- Completing the Required Learning Phase within the stated deadline.

- Respecting intellectual property and licensing requirements.

- Complying with all hackathon rules published by Elmahrosa.

Accepted participants will be asked to review and accept a **Participation Agreement** prior to the hackathon. This agreement will cover:

- Code of Conduct acknowledgment
